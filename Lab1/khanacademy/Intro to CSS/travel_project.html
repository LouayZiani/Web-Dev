Travel to Morocco

Why Morocco?

Morocco is a vibrant mix of culture, history, landscapes, and cuisine, offering everything from bustling souks in Marrakech to stargazing in the Sahara Desert. It’s affordable, accessible, and endlessly diverse, making it a destination that appeals to adventurers, food lovers, and history buffs alike.

Some History

Morocco’s history begins with the Amazigh peoples, the land’s first inhabitants whose culture still shapes the nation today. Coastal trading posts were later built by Phoenicians and Carthaginians, followed by Roman cities like Volubilis. In the 7th century, Arab armies brought Islam, and powerful dynasties such as the Almoravids and Almohads expanded Morocco’s influence across North Africa and Spain. Despite European pressures, Morocco remained independent longer than its neighbors, eventually becoming a French and Spanish protectorate in the early 20th century before regaining independence in 1956. Today, Morocco reflects this layered past, blending Amazigh, Arab, and Mediterranean traditions with modern life.
